Category 1: Farming


India gave $22.5 billion as input subsidy, including support for irrigation, fertilizers and electricity, in marketing year 2017-18. As per agricultural census for 2015-16, 99.43% of farm holdings are of lowincome or resource poor farmers.

Category 2: Dairy


25% of the outlay (33.33 % for SC / ST farmers) as back-ended capital subsidy subject to a ceiling of Rs 1.20 lakh for a unit of 20 calves ( Rs 1.60 lakh for SC/ST farmers). A maximum permissible capital subsidy is Rs 30,000 ( Rs 40,000 for SC/ST farmers) for a 5 calf unit.
